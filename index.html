Written Communication

Using Written Communication is key in modern times. First of all, this type of communication requires some primary education in order to understand and be understood. Beyond that, writing in a comprehensive way requires a complete high-school education, and even academy-level education.

Dear Domino's,
Why is my pizza late?
Signed, Chaim.
(an example of a letter)

Written Communication is used everywhere in modern societies, everyday. Books, emails, messages , etc. are all common ways of using written language. Around the world, there are many different languages, each used by a different group of people.

Ten Most spoken languages in the world, by declining order:

Verbal Communication

Languages are used both in Written Communication and Verbal Communication. Everyday life is filled with this type of communication, face-to-face, skype, discord, etc.

Michael: Hey Tim, How are you?
Tim: I'm doing alright, how about you?
Michael: Everything's fine.
(an example of a conversation)

Regardless of the type of conversation, it is not just about the words, it is also about the caliber and complexity of those words, and intonation.

Some types of intonation are:
  • Pitch
  • Tone
  • Cadence
Non-Verbal Communication

Listening

Listening is the most important aspect of communication.
It is key to all effective communication. Without the ability to listen effectively, messages are easily misunderstood. As a result, communication breaks down and the sender of the message can easily become frustrated or irritated.
Effective listening is a skill that underpins all positive human relationships.
